Static vs Dynamic

Static Frame

Locked. Silent. Apartment-safe.

Zero actuators. Zero motion. Still supports direct drive, loadcell, HOTAS, pedals, Stream Deck, quad display. Ideal if you share walls, rent, or don't want the seat to move under you mid-FPS.

Why: Cheaper entry. No floor drama. Feels like a pro workstation that can also lap Spa.

Dynamic Frame

Motion-capable. Built to take hits.

Actuator-ready bracing, reinforced crossmembers, mounting points for surge/heave/sway kits. Designed to translate telemetry into motion without twisting or blowing out alignment.

Why: Full-body race/flight cues. Physical G onset. You feel yaw, pitch, brake load, touchdown.

    Performance Tuning

    Lock comfort, unlock pace

    Seat & Reach

    Posture that lasts all night.

    • Seat back: 95–105°; hips slightly below knees.
    • Knees: ~110° in racing; ~100–110° in flight.
    • Arms: 15–30° bend at full lock or stick deflection.
    • Elbows low, shoulders relaxed; no shrugging to reach controls.

    Wheel / Yoke / Stick

    Precision beats strength.

    • Wheel/yoke height: top rim ≈ at or just below shoulder height.
    • Center-stick: between knees; elbow ≈ 90° at neutral; add extension if wrists bend.
    • Dual-stick: forearms parallel; wrists neutral; symmetrical reach.

    Pedals

    Control without cramp.

    • Racing: knee 110–120°, ankle 15–30° at rest; brake preload you can hold 60s.
    • Flight: heels supported; avoid toe-only posture; rudder travel clear of rails.

    Screen & FOV

    Clarity without motion sickness.

    • Top bezel ≈ eye height; center ~15° below eye line.
    • Distance: ~1.2× screen height (27″ ≈ 35–45 cm; 34″ ≈ 45–60 cm).
    • Triples: angle 45–60°; side edges just out of peripheral vision.
    • Use in-game FOV calculators; chase scale, not “wide”.

    Input Curves

    Smooth at center, control at the edge.

    • Flight sticks: curve 15–25, deadzone 2–3%.
    • Racing brake: linear or mild progressive; full travel without spike.
    • Mouse/FPS: stable pad, cable relief; LOD low; no desk wobble.

    VR Notes

    Comfort first, frames second.

    • Dial IPD physically, then software. Clear edges > raw sharpness.
    • Enable motion reprojection if FPS dips; prioritize CPU frametime.
    • Route cables high on frame; avoid neck tugs.

    Quick-Start

    1. Chassis: Dynamic.
    2. Loadout: Flight → add Dual Stick or Stick + Throttle, Flight Pedal Mount, Quad Monitor Setup.

    DCS

    Center-stick HOTAS + pedals.

    1. Chassis: Dynamic or Static (Static if neighbors).
    2. Loadout: Flight → Stick + Throttle, Flight Pedal Mount, Quad Monitor Setup for gauges/MFD.
    3. Curves: pitch/roll 20–30; rudder 10–20.
    4. Trim: stick should sit neutral, elbows ~90°.

    iRacing / ACC

    Wheel + pedals; triples or quad.

    1. Chassis: Dynamic if you want motion, Static if you just want planted torque.
    2. Loadout: Racing → Wheel Arm, Racing Pedal Mount, Gearshift Mount, Triple or Quad Monitor Setup.
    3. Brake: preload you can hold for 60s without shaking.
    4. FOV: use in-game calc; aim for true scale.

    FAQ

    Straight answers
    Will Dynamic shake my house apart?

    Dynamic is actuator-ready, but travel is tuned for cues — heave, surge, yaw onset — not theme-park throw. It's built to feel like seat-of-the-pants force, not bounce furniture across the floor.

    Can I start Static and upgrade later?

    Yes. Static → Dynamic is a supported path. You keep your controls, screens, mounts, Stream Deck arm — you’re not re-buying the whole rig.

    How long does assembly take?

    About 2 hours with a hex set. Modules click in — no drilling.

    Triple / Quad monitor really stable?

    Yes. Arms mount into frame structure, not a wobbly desk post. The overhead HUD stays where you put it, even under braking or pitch.
